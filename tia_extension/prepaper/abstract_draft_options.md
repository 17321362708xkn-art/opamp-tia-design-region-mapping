# Abstract Draft Options

These drafts are intentionally conservative and should be edited after related-work positioning and target-journal selection.

## Option 1: Conservative Portfolio / Report Style

Photodiode transimpedance amplifier design requires balancing feedback resistance, photodiode capacitance, feedback compensation, op-amp bandwidth, gain peaking, and output-noise trade-offs. This report-style pre-paper package presents a reproducible MATLAB behavioural workflow for early design-region screening under finite op-amp bandwidth constraints. The workflow computes baseline transimpedance response, sweeps feedback capacitance, extracts bandwidth and peaking metrics, and maps project-defined Safe, Marginal, and Risky regions over selected `Rf`, `Cpd`, `Cf`, and op-amp unity-gain-frequency assumptions. Real LTspice macromodel evidence is included for OP27, OPA818, and ADA4817, with processed CSV data and generated figures used to compare bandwidth and peaking trends across three vendor model sets. A first-pass behavioural noise analysis adds design context for relative noise-bandwidth trade-offs under stated assumptions. The package is intended as transparent pre-design screening evidence, not a hardware-validated circuit or measured-noise study. No hardware validation, measured-noise validation, or full Q3 submission readiness is claimed.

## Option 2: Q3 Pre-Paper Style

This work presents a MATLAB-and-SPICE-assisted workflow for photodiode transimpedance amplifier design-region mapping under finite op-amp bandwidth constraints. A behavioural MATLAB model is used to evaluate transimpedance magnitude, phase, bandwidth, and peaking across feedback resistance, photodiode capacitance, feedback capacitance, and op-amp unity-gain frequency. The resulting project-defined Safe, Marginal, and Risky regions provide an early-stage screening view of bandwidth and compensation trade-offs. The behavioural evidence is complemented by real LTspice macromodel comparisons for three vendor op-amp models: OP27, OPA818, and ADA4817. These comparisons show model-dependent bandwidth and peaking behaviour, including strong small-`Cf` peaking in the OP27 smoke test, stable selected OPA818 cases, and a borderline small-`Cf` ADA4817 case. First-pass behavioural noise estimates are added to connect compensation choices with relative output-noise trends. The workflow is reproducible through committed scripts, CSV source data, figures, and a figure manifest. The current package is a Q3 pre-paper prototype and does not claim hardware validation, measured-noise validation, or final submission readiness.

## Option 3: Stronger Journal-Style Draft

Early photodiode transimpedance amplifier design is complicated by coupled trade-offs among detector capacitance, feedback compensation, op-amp bandwidth, frequency-response peaking, and output noise. This paper draft proposes a reproducible design-region mapping workflow that combines MATLAB behavioural modelling with real vendor SPICE macromodel comparison evidence. The MATLAB model represents finite op-amp gain and unity-gain bandwidth, then sweeps `Rf`, `Cpd`, `Cf`, and `ft_Hz` to extract bandwidth and peaking metrics and assign project-defined Safe, Marginal, and Risky screening labels. Feedback-capacitance sweeps illustrate the expected bandwidth versus peaking trade-off, while design-region maps identify regions that merit closer vendor simulation. Real LTspice macromodel comparisons for OP27, OPA818, and ADA4817 provide three vendor-specific evidence sets and show that peaking and bandwidth boundaries depend on the selected macromodel and operating assumptions. First-pass behavioural noise analysis further contextualizes compensation choices through relative output-noise estimates. The workflow is positioned as a transparent pre-design screening method, not a new circuit topology or complete validation package. No hardware validation or measured-noise validation is claimed, and final Q3 submission readiness remains dependent on manuscript development and domain review.
