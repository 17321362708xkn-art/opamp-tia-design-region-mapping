\begin{abstract}
Early photodiode transimpedance amplifier (TIA) design requires coordinated screening of compensation, bandwidth, peaking, input-node capacitance, and noise assumptions before detailed circuit implementation. This work presents a reproducible early-stage photodiode TIA screening workflow that combines behavioural MATLAB sweeps, project-defined design-region mapping, quantified behavioural-vs-vendor-macromodel agreement checks, and first-pass behavioural noise estimates under clearly stated modelling assumptions. The behavioural model represents total input-node capacitance as $C_{\mathrm{total}} = C_{pd} + C_{in} + C_{\mathrm{stray}}$, with vendor-specific $C_{in}$ used only where documented and zero-valued defaults retained for backward compatibility. Across the selected OP27, OPA818, and ADA4817 vendor-export cases, 9 of 11 project-defined labels agree between the MATLAB behavioural model and vendor macromodel summaries. The OPA818 and ADA4817 high-speed cases show broad agreement for moderate and larger $C_f$ values, while the smallest-$C_f$ cases expose model sensitivity and the limits of the simplified single-pole behavioural approximation. The contribution is an engineering design workflow for photodiode TIA pre-design exploration, not a new TIA topology or universal design rule. The evidence is modelling and simulation evidence only; no hardware validation, measured-noise validation, complete SPICE coverage, or universal stability guarantee is claimed.
\end{abstract}
