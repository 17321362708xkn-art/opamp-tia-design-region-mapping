\begin{abstract}
Early photodiode transimpedance amplifier (TIA) design requires coordinated screening of compensation, bandwidth, peaking, and noise assumptions before detailed circuit implementation. This work presents a reproducible early-stage photodiode TIA screening workflow that combines behavioural MATLAB sweeps, project-defined design-region mapping, selected vendor macromodel comparisons, and first-pass behavioural noise estimates under clearly stated modelling assumptions. The workflow first establishes a finite-bandwidth behavioural baseline, then sweeps feedback capacitance to expose bandwidth and peaking trade-offs, maps project-defined Safe/Marginal/Risky screening regions across selected photodiode-capacitance and op-amp bandwidth assumptions, compares selected cases with OP27, OPA818, and ADA4817 vendor macromodel exports, and adds first-pass behavioural noise context. The contribution is an engineering design workflow for photodiode TIA pre-design exploration, not a new TIA topology or universal design rule. The evidence is modelling and simulation evidence only; no hardware validation, measured-noise validation, complete SPICE coverage, or universal stability guarantee is claimed.
\end{abstract}
